Oleksandr Roytdurd

Born: 1961, Odessa

Education:
In 1983 he graduated from the art and graphic faculty of the Odessa Pedagogical Institute.

Biography:
Since 1989 - he was a member of the National Union of Artists of Ukraine, is not currently the case. Representative of the Southern Ukrainian Art School, as well as postmodernism.

Since 1993 - co-founder of the association "New Art" (Odessa). From 1993 to 1999 he worked as an art director, and from 1999 to 2001 - the president of this association.

1997-1999 - Chairman of the Board of the Soros Center for Contemporary Art (Odesa).

2001-2002 - regional coordinator of the festival "Cultural heroes" (Odessa).

Since 2002 - Director of the Gelman Gallery in Kiev.




Participant of more than 100 exhibitions and artistic projects, among which:

  • Postanaestesia, 1992, Villa F. Stucca, Munich; Grassi Museum, Leipzig
  • Angels over Ukraine, 1993, Apostolic Church, Edinburgh
  • Configura 2,1995, Erfurt, Germany
  • The future - now, 1999, Museum of Contemporary Art, Zagreb, Croatia
  • When the screens become thinner, 1999, Passage de Reetz, Paris
  • Open ends, 2000, MoMA, New York
  • Humanity Plateau, 2001, 49th Venice Biennale, Venice
  • Screening, 2001, Spencer Brownstone Gallery, New York
  • Kunst und drogen, 2002, Rebellminds Gallery, Berlin
  • Girls, Nymphettes, Girls, Nymphettes, 2002, Rebellminds Gallery, Berlin
  • Teach Bad, 2010, Karas Gallery, Kyiv

Ivan Marchuk

Ivan Stepanovych Marchuk is a contemporary Ukrainian painter.

Born: May 12, 1936

Education:

Lviv Institute of Applied Art

Ivan Marchuk was born in a family of a weaver. He studied applied art in Lviv till 1965. Later he worked in Kyiv. In 1989 he left USSR and lived in the United States, Canada and Australia, where he became renowned. After the September 11, 2001 attacks which he saw, Marchuk returned to Kiev. Not having been valued in the Soviet Union, Marchuk was well accepted in the independent Ukraine obtaining a prestigious T. Shevchenko award in 1997. He is the only Ukrainian artist recognized by International Academy of Modern Art in Rome as the member of the “Golden Guild”. In 2007 The Daily Telegraph included Ivan Marchuk in top 100 geniuses of our time, who create a multidimensional picture of the Universe and, being ahead of time, determine trends and development perspectives of the postindustrial civilization.A museum of Ivan Marchuk is planned to be constructed in Kiev. His collection consists of about 5000 pieces of art in 12 creative series, some of which include:”Voice of My Soul”, “Landscape”, “ White Planet 1”, “A Glance into Infinity”. For more than 50 years of his artistic life the Master had 200 monographic and collective exhibitions, created thousands of colorful and monoсhrome works in his completely original technique «pliontanizm” (from Ukrainian word “pliontaty”- to weave, to wattle), which balances on the man-made and technological limits. The impact of his paintings has dramatic effect of 25 fast frames, that influences on the human sub consciousness, attracts and urges to changes. The Master created his own belief system by the artistic instruments, based on fine aesthetics and deep-rooted traditions, directed towards the long-term perspective.
